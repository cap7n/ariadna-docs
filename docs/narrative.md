# 01 · Narrative

> After killing her adoptive father, Ariadna becomes trapped in a labyrinth built by her own traumatized mind. To move forward, she has to face her fears.

## Premise

A young orphan with unusual potential is adopted by a stranger who becomes her adoptive father. He takes her aboard a spaceship and tells her, plainly, that her life means nothing: she is a pawn in a game that isn't hers. Over years he trains her, experiments on her, and tortures her, trying to force absolute loyalty.

During a combat-training session, Ariadna takes her father's pistol and shoots him. She turns his own words back on him:

!!! quote ""
    *"Your life means nothing… you can do nothing about it."*
    *"Now this pawn has your life in her hands."*

## The labyrinth

Looking for a way out, Ariadna finds the facility has become a maze. Rooms repeat. Corridors fold back on themselves. Her only emotional anchor is a lonely fish she keeps returning to.

Her father, **Oliwier**, reappears again and again, each time more monstrous. She kills him five times over what feels like years. Eventually she finds the aquarium empty. Alone with her fears, she confronts a final creature-form of her father and destroys it.

The monster collapses into dust. Light floods in through opening doors, and beyond them: mountains, trees, an airfield. A way out that was never a corridor.

## Cast

### Ariadna
The protagonist. Orphaned, gifted, and systematically broken by the man who adopted her. The labyrinth is her mind; every escalation is her fear made architecture.

### Oliwier Brytol
The adoptive father and the antagonist. A former leader of a radical faction that opposed governmental and corporate corruption, he abandoned his own extremists to run secret experiments, funded by means that are never made clear. In the labyrinth he is the boss: a manifestation of abuse rather than a conventional monster.

### The fish
Ariadna's single point of tenderness in the maze: a fragile anchor she returns to between horrors. When the aquarium finally turns up empty, the last soft thing is gone and only the final confrontation remains.

---

!!! info "Design intent"
    The narrative is delivered environmentally and through repetition, not cutscenes. The *five deaths* of Oliwier map onto the room-revisit and boss-tier systems; the story escalates because the mechanics escalate.
