# 09 · Environment

> Climate, space, material.

The environment carries the horror. The world starts **cold, clinical, technical** and degrades toward **oppressive and surreal** as the labyrinth asserts itself, a visual record of trauma rising to the surface.

## Visual direction

- **Clinical → oppressive.** Spaces begin as sterile facility interiors and warp into something wrong: too tall, too quiet, subtly non-Euclidean.
- **Palette.** Low-poly retro with film grain and oppressive contrast. The working tones are **fear-red**, **rust-amber**, **cold-memory** blues/greys, and **dark voids**.
- **Boss framing.** Heavy vertical form, low contrast: pressure over spectacle.
- **Gun assembly framing.** Ritualistic: metalwork, focused lighting, negative space.

## Material & mood

The section is anchored by a **hero concept image** and a **moodboard gallery** (twelve reference images) that lock the visual language for space and material. The mood, more than any single asset, is the deliverable: how a room *feels* to stand in matters more than its polycount.

## Authoring principle

Lighting, effects, and materials live in **scenes**, authored in the editor, so artists own the look directly. They are not generated in code. The runtime handles streaming and scaling; the *feel* of each room is hand-built.
